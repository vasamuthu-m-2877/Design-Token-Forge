# 00 — Thesis

## The one line

> **DTF turns any live product into a structured archetypal catalog, mapped
> against a design vocabulary you control.**

Everything else — tokens, components, Figma sync, archetype kits, drift
dashboards, AI generation, business model — is infrastructure around that one
move.

## The formula

A product is fully describable as a **finite set of archetypal regions**,
recurring across screens and states. Typical SaaS product: ~500 screens,
~100–150 distinct region archetypes.

If we can extract that vocabulary **completely** and **precisely**, we've
reduced any product to a structured catalog. Once it's structured, it is:

- **Measurable** — drift, coverage, consistency become numbers
- **Redesignable** — swap an archetype's implementation, the product updates
- **Rebuildable** — the catalog *is* the spec; any framework, any future tool
- **Onboardable** — a new designer reads the gallery, not the codebase
- **Generatable** — AI composes new screens from real product vocabulary

Every hard problem in product design — adoption, drift, redesigns, migrations,
brand consistency, design-system ROI — collapses into the same root: **nobody
has a complete, structured catalog of what their product actually contains.**

DTF builds that catalog.

## What DTF is *not*

- Not a Figma plugin (Figma is one surface, not the system)
- Not a component library (components are L1 — necessary but not sufficient)
- Not a token tool (tokens are L0 — physics, not product)
- Not a screenshot-to-code generator (generic output, no system grounding)
- Not a visual regression tool (diffs pixels, not archetypes)

## What DTF *is*

A **product → catalog → system compiler**.

- **Input:** a live product (HTML/CSS in production)
- **Output:** a complete structured catalog of every UI archetype, mapped
  against a design system's primitives, with drift scored and gaps named
- **Continuous:** re-run on every deploy; track fidelity over time

DTF is the **measurement and definition layer for product design fidelity**.
